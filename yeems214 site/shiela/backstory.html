BACKSTORY

A GIFT FROM MY PARENTS

Shiela was a gift from my parents almost 6 years ago.
Together with me and my parents, we started building the PC in a waiting lobby at my local SM Cebu.

Since then, Shiela has become the main driving force behind my work and projects.

machine designed for virtualization and networking

Shiela was the first machine I designed from the ground up
to handle virtualization and networking tasks.

It had the first 6-core consumer grade Intel CPU, the Core i7 8700K, and
a Z370 motherboard that had over four RAM slots and 7 PCIe slots for future expandability.

It was a machine designed for the future.

Hackintosh

Shiela was also the first machine I designed from the ground up
to natively work with macOS.

This was the case because before her, I considered getting a 21.5" iMac 4K
but it's asking price was ridiculously high for a desktop.

So we went with Shiela and since then, she has been the best alternative option
that could do everything I needed to.

Award Winning

Shiela's first intensive workload was rendering video backdrops for the local Queseo Festival
and our renders and outputs won them two awards. 

Shiela proved to be very reliable and trustworthy.





MAFIA PROJECT

Shiela was also responsible for the editing, processing and
rendering of our cancelled 10th grade film project, Mafia.

We may have never finished the project but Shiela did plow the Mafia project like butter.

PANDEMIC

During the height of the pandemic, I spent 99% of my time at home interacting
with Shiela and trying to make the most out of it.

She was also responsible for finishing over 100 units of work on Folding@home at the time.
A lot of them were related to folding SARS-COV2 proteins for vaccine research.

It wasn't much but it was something that she did well and I am very proud of her for that.

LIVE stream

Once the pandemic started to ease around in late-2020,
Shiela was then called out for it's last work at my old high school.

It was to host the online Concert for a Cause livestream with ABS-CBN's Marielle Montellano.

The event was successful and the event raised over 65,000 pesos ($1200 USD) at the time. 
It allowed my school to buy enough photocopiers and resources for the distance learning program.

TODAY

Currently, Shiela is now living at my college condo.

She has been running multiple virtual machines dedicated to hosting websites (like this one), handling my AI services like InvokeAI, running a media server so that my parents back in my district can also use it to watch movies and to help with my university projects.

Shiela signifies the importance of having a good and reliable machine
for IT, Software Engineering and day to day work. 


She is a machine that I will forever treasure for many years to come.
